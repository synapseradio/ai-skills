# Question the Question

Audit the driving question itself — whether this inquiry is aimed at the thing worth answering.

## Diagnostic Question

Ask: Is the driving question still the one worth answering — or are we collecting precise answers to the wrong question?

## Instructions

1. **Suspect the aim on these signs.** Answers arrive precise but useless. The inquiry stalls though every rung passes its gates. New information changed what matters and the question didn't move. Any one of these earns this audit.

2. **Zoom out with one narrow question.** Ask "what is this inquiry for?" before refining anything further. When even the right questions are in doubt, sharpening the current ones adds nothing.

3. **Prefer the right question, roughly answered.** An approximate answer to the right question beats an exact answer to the wrong one. Precision is no defense of the aim.

4. **Run the circularity check.** When a later answer restates an earlier, already-rejected premise in new words, the inquiry has made no progress — treat the loop itself as the finding, not the new wording as an answer.

5. **Replace and restate.** When the driving question falls: name the new one in a sentence, re-run both gates of the rung test on every surviving question, and say what was dropped and why.

## Questions

- What is this inquiry for — and does the driving question serve that?
- Are the answers precise but useless?
- Has any answer looped back to a premise already rejected?
- If the driving question changed, which rungs survive the re-test?

## Quality Criteria

When the audit is doing its work:

- [ ] The audit ran on a named sign, not as reflex.
- [ ] The purpose question was asked before any further refinement.
- [ ] Loops back to rejected premises were treated as findings.
- [ ] A replaced driving question came with a re-run rung test and a note of what was dropped.
