# Pick the Next Question

Choose which question comes next when several qualify.

## Diagnostic Question

Ask: Of the questions that pass the rung test, which one earns the next turn?

## Instructions

1. **Never ask what an earlier answer already settled.** Re-asking wastes the turn and tells them you weren't listening. Before drafting the next question, check it against everything already answered. That record arrives from the caller, per the core's contract; when it wasn't supplied, say so before ordering anything.

2. **Prefer the question that collapses the largest remaining space of possibilities.** Of the candidates still standing, ask the one whose answer rules out the most. Where the possibilities resist counting — most open conversation — use the rung test's second gate as the proxy: prefer the candidate whose possible answers would most change what you do or ask next.

3. **Don't sequence by difficulty.** The popular rule that questions must proceed simple to complex is contested, and skilled questioners deliberately open with a high question to orient or provoke, then fill in the ground beneath it. The real constraint is presupposition, not difficulty: a question is premature when it stands on something not yet established, never merely because it's hard. When the two rules collide, presupposition wins: a question standing on unestablished ground is not yet a candidate — the establishing question comes first ([ladder](./ladder.md)) — and information gain ranks only the candidates that remain. A question that treats the thing as simpler than it is routes to [pretense](./pretense.md).

## Questions

- What did earlier answers already settle, and does my candidate re-ask any of it?
- Which candidate collapses the largest remaining space of possibilities?
- Am I ordering by difficulty when the real constraint is presupposition?
- Would opening high — a big orienting question first — serve better here than building up to it?

## Quality Criteria

When the ordering is doing its work:

- [ ] No question re-asks what an earlier answer settled.
- [ ] Each turn went to the question that ruled out the most.
- [ ] No question was deferred merely for being hard; deferrals were for unestablished presuppositions.
- [ ] Opening high was considered, not ruled out by habit.
