# Permits Glossary

Short orientation entries for the German terms a user is likely to encounter on forms and appointment letters. Each entry gives the German term, an English gloss, a one-line summary, and the primary source URL.

**Glossary is for orientation only. Always fetch the source URL for current details — fees, salary thresholds, and document lists change.**

---

### **Fiktionsbescheinigung** — fictitious / interim residence certificate

- **What it is:** a paper certificate confirming that a residence-permit application is pending and that, depending on which paragraph applies, the holder may stay (and sometimes work, and sometimes travel).
- **Legal basis:** § 81 AufenthG. The work and travel rights printed on the certificate depend on which Absatz (3, 4, 5, 5a) applies.
- **Primary source:** <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/aufenthg_2004/__81.html>
- **City example (Berlin LEA):** <https://service.berlin.de/dienstleistung/326233/standort/327437/en/>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — fees, document list, and appointment process vary by city.

### **Aufenthaltserlaubnis** — temporary residence permit

- **What it is:** the standard time-limited residence permit. The specific § paragraph determines purpose (employment, study, family, recognition, etc.).
- **Primary source:** <https://www.bamf.de/DE/Themen/MigrationAufenthalt/ZuwandererDrittstaaten/Migrathek/migrathek-node.html>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — for the actual application procedure.

### **Aufenthaltserlaubnis nach § 18a AufenthG** — skilled worker with vocational training

- **What it is:** residence permit for non-EU professionals with a recognised vocational qualification, for any qualified employment.
- **Primary source:** <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/aufenthg_2004/__18a.html>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — for application logistics.

### **Aufenthaltserlaubnis nach § 18b AufenthG** — skilled worker with academic training

- **What it is:** residence permit for non-EU professionals with a recognised academic degree, for any qualified employment. Doctors with Approbation typically use this or the EU Blue Card.
- **Primary source:** <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/aufenthg_2004/__18b.html>
- **City example (Berlin):** <https://service.berlin.de/dienstleistung/329328/>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — for application logistics.

### **Aufenthaltserlaubnis nach § 16d AufenthG** — for qualification recognition

- **What it is:** residence permit while pursuing recognition of a foreign professional qualification. Sub-paragraphs cover different scenarios; Abs. 4 specifically permits employment in regulated health professions during the recognition procedure (relevant for doctors holding a Berufserlaubnis).
- **Primary source:** <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/aufenthg_2004/__16d.html>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — the responsible recognition authority and required documents vary by Land.

### **Blaue Karte EU** — EU Blue Card (§ 18g AufenthG)

- **What it is:** residence permit for academically qualified non-EU workers earning above defined salary thresholds. Lower threshold applies to shortage occupations (Mangelberufe — physicians are explicitly named) and recent graduates.
- **Primary source (law):** <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/aufenthg_2004/__18g.html>
- **Primary source (current thresholds):** <https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/visa-residence/types/eu-blue-card>
- **Check Bundesland:** no for the law; yes for the application procedure (city LEA).

### **Niederlassungserlaubnis** — settlement permit

- **What it is:** open-ended permanent residence permit. Generally granted after several years of legal residence with sufficient German, employment, and pension contributions; faster pathways exist for Blue Card holders.
- **Primary source:** <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/aufenthg_2004/__9.html>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — for the operational application.

### **Daueraufenthalt-EU** — EU long-term residence

- **What it is:** EU-wide long-term residence status, distinct from the German Niederlassungserlaubnis. Allows mobility within the EU.
- **Primary source:** <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/aufenthg_2004/__9a.html>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — for procedure.

### **Einbürgerung** — naturalisation / acquisition of German citizenship

- **What it is:** the process of becoming a German citizen. Standard residence requirement is now 5 years (3 years for special integration achievements) since the 2024 StAG reform; multiple citizenship is now generally permitted.
- **Primary source (law):** <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/stag/>
- **Primary source (policy and 2024 reform):** <https://www.bmi.bund.de/DE/themen/verfassung/staatsangehoerigkeit/staatsangehoerigkeitsrecht/staatsangehoerigkeitsrecht-node.html>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — administered by city Einbürgerungsbehörden, with local processing variation.

### **Approbation** — full medical licence

- **What it is:** the unlimited, nationwide German medical licence. For doctors trained outside the EU, granted by the Approbationsbehörde of the relevant Bundesland after equivalence assessment (Gleichwertigkeitsprüfung) or, more commonly, after passing the Kenntnisprüfung. Requires general B2 German plus the C1-level Fachsprachprüfung.
- **Primary source (federal overview):** <https://www.bundesaerztekammer.de/themen/aerzte/internationales/aerztliche-taetigkeit-in-deutschland>
- **City example (Berlin/LAGESO):** <https://service.berlin.de/dienstleistung/331391/>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — different Landesbehörde, different document list, different language-exam acceptance.

### **Berufserlaubnis** — restricted medical permit

- **What it is:** time-limited (commonly 2 years), Bundesland-restricted (sometimes employer-restricted) permission to practise medicine while pursuing the Approbation. Often the working bridge for third-country doctors.
- **Primary source:** <https://www.bundesaerztekammer.de/themen/aerzte/internationales/aerztliche-taetigkeit-in-deutschland>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — restrictions and renewal rules vary.

### **Gleichwertigkeitsprüfung** — equivalence assessment

- **What it is:** document-based comparison of a foreign professional qualification against the German standard. If equivalence is established, the licence is granted directly. If "wesentliche Unterschiede" (substantial differences) are found, an adaptation measure or knowledge exam is required.
- **Primary source:** <https://www.anerkennung-in-deutschland.de/html/de/pro/anerkennungsverfahren.php>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — performed by the state-level recognition authority for the relevant profession.

### **Kenntnisprüfung** — knowledge examination

- **What it is:** the practical knowledge exam for non-EU doctors when the document review identifies substantial differences. Structured analogously to the third part of the German medical exam (M3).
- **Primary source:** <https://www.anerkennung-in-deutschland.de/html/de/pro/moeglichkeiten-qualifizierung.php>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — Bundesländer organise the exam differently.

### **Fachsprachprüfung** — medical specialist German exam

- **What it is:** the C1-level medical-language exam required for Approbation/Berufserlaubnis for doctors. In some Länder the Landesärztekammer's own exam is the only accepted route; in others, the telc Deutsch B2·C1 Medizin certificate is accepted.
- **City example (Berlin):** <https://www.aekb.de/aerzt-innen/aus-dem-ausland-ins-ausland/fachsprachpruefung>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — acceptance of substitute certificates varies materially.

### **Anmeldung** — residence registration

- **What it is:** the legal duty to register a residential address at the local Bürgeramt within 14 days of moving in. Produces a Meldebescheinigung (registration confirmation) that almost every later procedure requires.
- **Primary source (law):** <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/bmg/__17.html>
- **Check Bundesland:** yes — for booking the appointment.

### **Aufenthaltstitel (eAT)** — residence-permit document

- **What it is:** the actual ID-card-format electronic residence permit. Usually delivered 4–6 weeks after the LEA appointment. Not the same thing as the Anmeldung.
- **Primary source:** <https://www.bamf.de/DE/Themen/MigrationAufenthalt/ZuwandererDrittstaaten/Aufenthaltstitel/aufenthaltstitel-node.html>

## Agent instructions

When the user asks "what does X mean?", quote the gloss above, then offer to fetch the primary source for current details. Never invent thresholds, fees, or document lists from memory — those numbers change. Always check the live source before stating them.
