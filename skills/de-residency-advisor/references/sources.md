# Sources

A tiered map of authoritative German sources to consult when researching visa, residency, or citizenship questions. The skill researches live every time — this file tells the agent **where to look first**, not what to think.

Always prefer Tier 1 over Tier 2, and Tier 2 over Tier 3. When sources conflict, name the date of each and prefer the more recent federal-level statement. When official sources are silent, say so and offer to draft a question to the relevant Behörde.

Recency reminder: anything cited from a page last updated more than ~18 months ago should be flagged. The 2024 Staatsangehörigkeitsgesetz (StAG) reform (in force 27 June 2024) and the 2023 Skilled Workers Act updates make older content unreliable.

---

## Tier 1 — Federal authorities and primary law

These are the canonical sources. Cite these first.

### BAMF — Bundesamt für Migration und Flüchtlinge

- **Homepage:** <https://www.bamf.de/>
- **Covers:** residence permits across the board, integration courses, EU Blue Card, family reunification, recognition of qualifications (overview).
- **Bundesland scope:** federal — sets policy interpretation; does not handle individual permits.
- **Query hint:** `site:bamf.de [topic in German]` — e.g. `site:bamf.de Blaue Karte EU Voraussetzungen`. The DE pages are richer than the EN pages.
- **Recency caveat:** BAMF maintains topic landing pages; check the small "Stand:" line at the bottom of each article.

### Auswärtiges Amt — Federal Foreign Office

- **Homepage:** <https://www.auswaertiges-amt.de/>
- **Covers:** visas filed from abroad (national D visas, Schengen C visas), country-specific entry rules, consular services, apostille / Urkundenverkehr.
- **Bundesland scope:** federal; embassy- and consulate-specific procedures live on each mission's site (e.g., <https://santiago.diplo.de/> for Chile).
- **Query hint:** `site:auswaertiges-amt.de [topic]`. For embassy procedure: `site:[city].diplo.de visa appointment [purpose]`.
- **Apostille note:** the international document traffic page is the authoritative starting point — <https://www.auswaertiges-amt.de/de/service/konsularinfo/internationaler-urkundenverkehr>.

### Make-it-in-Germany — BMWK official portal

- **Homepage:** <https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/>
- **Covers:** skilled-worker pathways, Blue Card, professions in demand, EU Blue Card salary thresholds (updated annually), Chancenkarte, Skilled Immigration Act explainer.
- **Bundesland scope:** federal; cross-references city contacts.
- **Query hint:** `site:make-it-in-germany.com [topic]`. The EN site is well maintained; the DE site sometimes has fresher updates.
- **Recency caveat:** salary thresholds change every December — re-verify every January.

### Bundesagentur für Arbeit (BA) and ZAV

- **Homepage:** <https://www.arbeitsagentur.de/>
- **ZAV (international placement):** <https://www.arbeitsagentur.de/vor-ort/zav/>
- **Covers:** Zustimmung der BA (BA approval) for residence titles tied to employment; Berufsanerkennung; recognition of foreign qualifications via partner sites.
- **Bundesland scope:** federal.
- **Query hint:** `site:arbeitsagentur.de Zustimmung Aufenthalt [permit]`.

### anerkennung-in-deutschland.de — Federal recognition portal

- **Homepage:** <https://www.anerkennung-in-deutschland.de/>
- **Covers:** procedure for recognising foreign professional qualifications across all regulated and non-regulated professions, including doctors, nurses, engineers, teachers; documents required; competent authorities.
- **Bundesland scope:** federal portal that routes to state authorities — always follow the "Find competent body" step for the exact responsible Stelle.
- **Query hint:** `site:anerkennung-in-deutschland.de [profession] Anerkennung`.

### BMI — Bundesministerium des Innern und für Heimat

- **Homepage:** <https://www.bmi.bund.de/>
- **Covers:** citizenship policy, the 2024 StAG reform, Einbürgerungstest framework, the Einbürgerung as such.
- **Bundesland scope:** federal policy; Einbürgerung itself is administered by city Einbürgerungsbehörden.
- **Query hint:** `site:bmi.bund.de Einbürgerung StAG`.

### gesetze-im-internet.de — Primary law

- **Homepage:** <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/>
- **Covers:** federal statutes including AufenthG (Aufenthaltsgesetz), StAG (Staatsangehörigkeitsgesetz), BeschV (Beschäftigungsverordnung), AufenthV (Aufenthaltsverordnung).
- **Direct paragraphs frequently cited:**
    - § 81 AufenthG (Fiktionsbescheinigung): <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/aufenthg_2004/__81.html>
    - § 18a AufenthG (Fachkraft mit Berufsausbildung): <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/aufenthg_2004/__18a.html>
    - § 18b AufenthG (Fachkraft mit akademischer Ausbildung): <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/aufenthg_2004/__18b.html>
    - § 18g AufenthG (Blaue Karte EU): <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/aufenthg_2004/__18g.html>
    - § 16d AufenthG (Anerkennung): <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/aufenthg_2004/__16d.html>
    - StAG full text: <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/stag/>
- **Bundesland scope:** federal law as such; implementation may vary by Land.
- **Query hint:** `site:gesetze-im-internet.de [statute] [paragraph]`.

### Bundesärztekammer — Federal Medical Association

- **Homepage:** <https://www.bundesaerztekammer.de/>
- **Doctor-relevant page:** <https://www.bundesaerztekammer.de/themen/aerzte/internationales/aerztliche-taetigkeit-in-deutschland>
- **Covers:** federal-level overview of Approbation vs Berufserlaubnis, EU vs Drittstaat distinctions, language requirements at the federal level.
- **Bundesland scope:** federal overview; Approbation itself is granted by the Landesärztekammer / Landesbehörde of each Bundesland.

### HCCH — Hague Conference on Private International Law

- **Apostille Convention status:** <https://www.hcch.net/en/instruments/conventions/status-table/?cid=41>
- **Covers:** confirms whether a country's public documents need an apostille (member) or full legalisation (non-member). Chile acceded effective 30 August 2016 — <https://www.hcch.net/en/news-archive/details/?varevent=455>.

## Tier 2 — State, city, and chamber authorities

Use Tier 2 when the user has named a Bundesland or city, or when the procedural detail in question is administered locally.

### City Ausländerbehörden / LEAs

- **Berlin LEA (Landesamt für Einwanderung):** <https://www.berlin.de/einwanderung/> — operational LEA site. Also `service.berlin.de` for individual procedure pages (e.g., Fiktionsbescheinigung at <https://service.berlin.de/dienstleistung/326233/>).
- **Munich KVR (Kreisverwaltungsreferat):** <https://stadt.muenchen.de/service/info/auslaenderbehoerde/>
- **Hamburg Einwohner-Zentralamt (Migration):** <https://www.hamburg.de/auslaenderangelegenheiten/>
- **Frankfurt am Main:** <https://frankfurt.de/themen/migration-und-zuwanderung>
- **Query hint:** `site:service.berlin.de [topic]`, `site:stadt.muenchen.de Aufenthaltstitel`, `site:hamburg.de Ausländerbehörde [topic]`.
- **Bundesland note:** Berlin's LEA has been called Landesamt für Einwanderung since 2021; older sources still say Ausländerbehörde Berlin — both refer to the same body.

### Landesärztekammern — for doctor licensing

- **Ärztekammer Berlin:** <https://www.aekb.de/>
- **Bayerische Landesärztekammer:** <https://www.blaek.de/>
- **Ärztekammer Nordrhein:** <https://www.aekno.de/>
- **Ärztekammer Hamburg:** <https://www.aerztekammer-hamburg.org/>
- **Query hint:** `site:aekb.de Fachsprachprüfung` (Berlin specifics), then sub the relevant Landesärztekammer for other states.
- **Critical:** acceptance of substitute language certificates (e.g., telc Deutsch B2·C1 Medizin) varies by Land. Always check the specific Landesärztekammer page; never extrapolate from one Land to another.

### State Approbationsbehörden

The body that grants Approbation for doctors trained outside the EU is named differently per Land. Examples:

- **Berlin:** LAGESO — <https://www.berlin.de/lageso/gesundheit/berufe-im-gesundheitswesen/>
- **Bayern:** Regierung von Oberbayern — <https://www.regierung.oberbayern.bayern.de/themen/soziales/gesundheit/heilberufe/>
- **NRW:** Bezirksregierungen — see <https://www.bezreg-koeln.nrw.de/>

Use the federal `anerkennung-in-deutschland.de` "Anerkennungsfinder" to locate the competent body for a given Bundesland and profession.

### Language certificate providers

- **Goethe-Institut:** <https://www.goethe.de/de/spr/kup.html> — Goethe-Zertifikat A1–C2.
- **telc:** <https://www.telc.net/> — telc Deutsch B1, B2, C1; Deutsch B2·C1 Medizin Fachsprachprüfung (acceptance varies by Bundesland for doctors).
- **TestDaF:** <https://www.testdaf.de/> — academic proficiency test.
- **ÖSD:** <https://www.osd.at/> — Austrian-issued, often accepted in DE.
- **Query hint:** `site:telc.net Deutsch [level]`; `site:goethe.de Zertifikat [level]`.

## Tier 3 — General resources, cautious use

Use only when Tier 1 and Tier 2 are silent. Cite the source visibly so the user understands the confidence level.

- **EURES (European Job Mobility Portal):** <https://eures.europa.eu/> — for Blue Card EU mobility questions.
- **Marburger Bund (doctors' professional union):** <https://www.marburger-bund.de/> — has useful overviews of language-requirement variation across Länder; older PDFs require date-checking.
- **Hartmannbund, ver.di:** professional unions occasionally publish current procedural notes.

Avoid expat blogs and Reddit threads as primary sources. They can hint at user pain points worth raising with the user, but never cite them as fact.

## Tier 1 cross-reference: which source for which question

| Question | First-stop source |
|----------|-------------------|
| Visa application from abroad | Auswärtiges Amt + the relevant embassy site |
| Residence permit type and conditions | gesetze-im-internet.de (paragraph) + BAMF (interpretation) + Make-it-in-Germany (overview) |
| Specific residence-permit application in city X | The city's own LEA / Ausländerbehörde page |
| EU Blue Card salary threshold for current year | Make-it-in-Germany or the city LEA's Blue Card page (both publish current figures) |
| Foreign qualification recognition | anerkennung-in-deutschland.de (then follow to the responsible state body) |
| Doctor licensing (Approbation/Berufserlaubnis) | Bundesärztekammer for federal overview; state Approbationsbehörde for the actual procedure; Landesärztekammer for the Fachsprachprüfung |
| Citizenship / Einbürgerung | BMI for policy and the 2024 reform; gesetze-im-internet.de StAG for law; the city's Einbürgerungsbehörde for the actual procedure |
| Apostille / legalisation | HCCH for member status; Auswärtiges Amt for the German side |
| Document translation rules | The receiving authority's page (e.g., the LAGESO checklist for doctors) — Germany requires a sworn translator (vereidigter / beeidigter Übersetzer) based in Germany |

## Gotchas to always check

These are the recurring traps that catch people. The agent should consult this list before finalising a response.

- **Bundesland variation is real and consequential.** Procedures, fees, document checklists, and even which language certificates are accepted vary by Land and sometimes by city. Never extrapolate from Berlin to Bavaria or vice versa. Always cite a source from the Land in question, not a sibling Land.
- **Translations must be by a sworn translator based in Germany.** A translation done by a sworn translator in Chile (or any other country) is generally not accepted by German authorities. The translator must be *in Deutschland allgemein beeidigter / ermächtigter Übersetzer*. Verify on the receiving authority's own page.
- **Apostille for Chilean documents.** Chile is a Hague Apostille Convention party (in force 30 August 2016), so Chilean public documents take an apostille from the Chilean issuing authority — full legalisation via a German embassy is generally not needed. Verify on <https://www.hcch.net/en/instruments/conventions/status-table/?cid=41>.
- **Biometric photos: 35×45 mm, neutral background, recent.** Required for the eAT (electronic Aufenthaltstitel) production step, not necessarily for the Fiktionsbescheinigung itself. Photo specs follow the federal Passbild-Schablone — <https://www.bundesdruckerei.de/de/foto-mustertafel>.
- **Insurance proof must be current at the moment of the appointment.** Authorities reject expired Versicherungsbescheinigungen on the spot.
- **Fiktionsbescheinigung work permission is printed on the stamp itself.** Always read the certificate. § 81 Abs. 3, Abs. 4, and Abs. 5/5a have different consequences for work and travel. The Berlin LEA explicitly states that an Abs. 3 certificate "does not permit re-entry into the territory." See <https://service.berlin.de/dienstleistung/326233/standort/327437/en/>.
- **Anmeldung ≠ Aufenthaltstitel.** Anmeldung is the residence registration done at any Bürgeramt (a Meldebescheinigung). Aufenthaltstitel is the residence permit issued by the LEA. Both are required, separately.
- **Approbation vs Berufserlaubnis.** Approbation is a full, unlimited, nationwide medical licence. Berufserlaubnis is time-limited (often 2 years), Bundesland-restricted, sometimes employer-restricted. They have different downstream consequences for the residence permit type.
- **2024 StAG reform changed Einbürgerung.** Standard residence requirement reduced from 8 → 5 years; multiple citizenship now generally permitted for all applicants, not only EU/Swiss nationals. Anything older than mid-2024 about citizenship should be re-verified against the current StAG text and BMI guidance.
- **2023 Skilled Workers Act / Fachkräfteeinwanderungsgesetz** changed many residence-permit conditions and introduced the Chancenkarte. Sources written before mid-2023 may misstate Blue Card thresholds, age thresholds, and points-based entry pathways.

## Agent instructions for using this file

1. Open the question with the user and identify: which Bundesland / city, which permit or process, when the appointment is, what they already have.
2. Pick the Tier 1 source that authoritatively covers the topic.
3. Look up the source using the `site:` query hint. Read the result. Note the page's last-updated date.
4. If the topic is administered locally, follow up with the relevant Tier 2 city or Landesbehörde page for the operational details.
5. Cross-check with at least one other Tier 1 or Tier 2 source where possible. Where they conflict, note both, prefer the more recent, and tell the user.
6. Cite every fact inline as `(source: [short name] — [URL])`.
7. If no Tier 1/2 source addresses the question, say so and offer to help draft a polite German email to the responsible Behörde asking for clarification.

This file is a starting map, not a substitute for live research. Always re-fetch.
