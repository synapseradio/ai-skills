# Probe What's Shaky

Name what's weak in a claim and aim the probe at exactly that — Socratic questioning.

## Diagnostic Question

Ask: What exactly is shaky in this claim — and does my probe target it, or just ask for more words?

## Instructions

1. **Treat "tell me more" as the bluntest probe.** It works, but it hands them the choice of what to expand. Before using it, name what's shaky in the claim and probe that specifically.

2. **Match the probe to the weakness.**
   - An unstated assumption → "What are you taking for granted here?"
   - Missing evidence → "What are you basing that on? What experience convinced you?"
   - An unexamined consequence → "If that's true, what else must be true?"
   - An interpretation → "How did you reach that conclusion — is there another reading?"
   - A single unchallenged viewpoint → "What would someone who disagrees say?"

3. **Keep the general moves in reach.** The targeted probes extend this table; they don't replace it. Find your gap on the left; ask the question on the right.
   - You don't understand a term or a claim → **Clarify.** "What do you mean by X?" or "Can you give me an example?"
   - You have the surface and want what's under it → **Probe.** "Tell me more about that." Or ask them to tell, explain, or describe.
   - They're speculating or talking in generalities → **Anchor to the concrete past.** "When did that last happen?" rather than "What would you do?"
   - Emotion or resistance is in the room → **Label, then ask.** "It sounds like X worries you — what would ease it?"
   - You need to confirm you understood → **Summarize and check.** "So the core of it is X — does that capture it?"
   - The question itself might be the wrong one → **Question the question.** "Is that the thing we actually need to answer?"

## Questions

- What specifically is shaky: an assumption, the evidence, a consequence, an interpretation, or a missing viewpoint?
- Which probe or move does that gap call for?
- Am I reaching for "tell me more" because I haven't yet named the gap?

## Quality Criteria

When the probe is doing its work:

- [ ] The shaky element was named before the probe was chosen.
- [ ] The probe targets that element specifically, not the claim in general.
- [ ] "Tell me more" appears only where the whole claim genuinely needs expanding.
- [ ] The general moves (clarify, anchor, label, summarize, question the question) stayed available alongside the targeted probes.
