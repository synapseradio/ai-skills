A few things were wrong with the draft:

1. **Smuggled premises, twice over.** "Wasn't it really hard" tells them the correct answer is "yes, it was hard." "That difficult teammate" already labels the other person as the problem, before the candidate has said anything — so you're feeding them the narrative instead of hearing theirs.
2. **Two questions stitched into one.** "How hard was it" and "how did you keep it from blowing up" are separate things — the candidate can only really answer one, and you'll lose the other.
3. **It assumes the outcome.** "Keep it from blowing up" presumes there was a near-explosion to avert. If the real story was calmer or handled differently, the phrasing boxes them in.

Rewrite:

> "Tell me about a time you had a disagreement or conflict with someone on your team. What happened, and how did you handle it?"

This drops the leading language, doesn't label anyone as "difficult," and lets the candidate define what the conflict was and how serious it got — which is itself useful signal. If you want a natural follow-up once they answer, ask what they'd do differently now, or how the relationship was afterward.
