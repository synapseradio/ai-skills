Classify current understanding into four quadrants: verified facts, known unknowns (questions you know to ask), unrecognized knowledge (expertise or data you have but have not applied here), and blind spots (what you do not know you do not know). For each piece of knowledge, identify its source: direct observation, documentation, inference, or assumption. Weigh readiness against risk — compare the cost of being wrong against the effort of verification. For known unknowns, name the question. For blind spots, use probes, analogies, or outsider perspectives to surface them.
