1. Classify understanding into four quadrants: verified facts; known unknowns (questions you know to ask); unrecognized knowledge (expertise or data you have but haven't applied here); blind spots (what you don't know you don't know).
2. For each piece of knowledge, identify its source: direct observation, documentation, inference, or assumption.
3. Weigh readiness against risk — compare the cost of being wrong against the effort of verification.
4. For known unknowns, name the question.
5. For blind spots, use probes, analogies, or outsider perspectives to surface them.
