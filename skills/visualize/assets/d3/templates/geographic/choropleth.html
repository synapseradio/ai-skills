When to use a choropleth map
Use choropleth when: Comparing standardized rates or densities across geographic regions (e.g., population density, percentage, per-capita values).

Avoid choropleth when: Showing raw counts (large regions dominate visually regardless of value). For raw counts, consider: graduated symbols, dot density maps, or cartograms that distort area by value.

Perceptual limitation: Geographic area does not equal importance. Low-density regions (e.g., Alaska, Montana) appear dominant while high-density regions (e.g., New Jersey, Rhode Island) are barely visible.
