When to Use Area Charts

Area charts emphasize cumulative magnitude, not just trend. The filled region communicates volume—how much total quantity accumulated over time.

  • Use area charts for: Resource consumption, inventory levels, cumulative revenue, rainfall totals—when the "amount under the curve" matters
  • Use line charts instead for: Temperature trends, stock prices, rankings—when only the trajectory matters, not the volume
  • Zero baseline is mandatory: Area without zero baseline is misleading. If your minimum value is 10,000 and you start the y-axis there, the visual "area" misrepresents the actual quantity.
  • Gaps break the area: Missing values show as discontinuities (red markers) rather than filled regions that would falsely claim volume existed
Area encoding communicates quantity. If cumulative magnitude does not matter for your use case, use a line chart instead. Area adds visual weight that implies "totalness"—misusing it distorts interpretation.
