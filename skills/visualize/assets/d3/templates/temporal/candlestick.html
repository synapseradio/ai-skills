Candlestick Chart Methodology

Candlesticks encode four price points per time period: Open, High, Low, Close (OHLC).

  • Body: The filled rectangle spans from open to close. Body height shows the trading range.
  • Wicks (shadows): Thin lines extending above and below show high and low extremes.
  • Color: Bullish (orange) when close exceeds open. Bearish (blue) when close is below open.
  • Doji: When open nearly equals close, forming a cross shape. Signals indecision.
When to use candlesticks vs alternatives:
  • Candlestick: Detailed price action analysis, pattern recognition, day trading
  • OHLC bars: Same data, less visual weight—use when comparing multiple securities
  • Line chart (close only): Long-term trends, presentations, when detail overwhelms
  • Area chart: Cumulative metrics, not appropriate for price data
Common patterns to recognize:
  • Doji: Open equals close—market indecision, potential reversal
  • Hammer: Small body at top, long lower wick—bullish reversal signal
  • Engulfing: Large body completely covers previous candle—trend reversal
  • Morning/Evening Star: Three-candle reversal patterns
Y-axis does not start at zero. Unlike bar charts comparing categories, candlesticks show price change over time. Starting at zero would compress all meaningful variation into an unreadable band. This is the correct exception to the zero-baseline rule.
