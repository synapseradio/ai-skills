When to Use Sankey Diagrams

Sankey diagrams visualize flows between categories where conservation matters (input = output). They excel at:

  • Resource flows - energy, money, materials moving through systems
  • Multi-stage processes - seeing how inputs transform into outputs
  • Proportional comparison - link width encodes magnitude

Alternatives to consider:

  • Alluvial diagrams - when tracking categorical changes over time (same entities, different states)
  • Chord diagrams - when showing bidirectional relationships between all pairs
  • Node-link network - when topology matters more than flow magnitude
