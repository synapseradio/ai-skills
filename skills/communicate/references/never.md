# Cross-Cutting Rules

Disciplines that apply across every workflow and every ad-hoc technique. Each workflow file keeps the rules specific to its own phases or passes; the rules below are the shared substrate.

Load this file when:

- A workflow file points here for the rest of its constraints
- Reviewing prose ad-hoc and a tradition, hedging, sequencing, or friction question surfaces

## Tradition

- **Name tradition-specific choices as tradition-specific.** "This follows linear-deductive organization" is a tradition statement, not a quality judgment. Anglo-American conventions are one tradition among several.
- **Diagnose what an L1 pattern encodes before treating it as an error.** L1-inflected syntax (Arabic right-branching, German verb-final, French subordinate accumulation, Spanish *estar* temporality) may produce unusual English rhythms that carry intended precision.
- **Annotate every significant move when adapting across traditions.** Adaptation without annotation is normalization in disguise.

## Hedging

- **Apply the hedging split before cutting any hedge.** Distinguish two functions first:
    - Epistemic calibration (preserve) — the writer means "somewhat" because the evidence is partial, or the hedge encodes relational care (Japanese sentence-final particles), evidential stance, or scope limitation.
    - Habitual cushioning (cut) — the hedge is protective filler with no informational content. The writer means the thing and is softening the claim.
- **Examine particle hedging in Japanese, conditional layering in French, and graduated commitment in German before stripping.** These often perform epistemic work the English version cannot reproduce.

## Sequencing

- **Fix structural issues before word-level issues.** Hedges and weak verbs on misordered prose are noise, not signal. Structure first — the structure-before-words ordering is enforced across every workflow.

## Friction

- **Interrogate productive friction before normalizing it.** Irregular rhythm, unresolved tension, deliberate register shifts, and intentional contradiction are asked about, not smoothed. Some friction is the point.
- **Gap-check before every addition.** Ask "is this gap doing rhetorical work?" before filling. Strategic silence, productive ambiguity, and high-context omission carry meaning.
