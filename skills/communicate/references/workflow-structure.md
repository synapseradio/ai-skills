# Structure Workflow

Adapt argument architecture across rhetorical traditions when the prose is already in the target language. Content, voice, and vocabulary stay; organization, positional conventions, and explicitness calibration shift.

**Boundary with the Bridge workflow:** Structure operates on prose already in the target language whose argument shape follows another tradition. Bridge operates on thoughts that live in another language and need rendering. If both apply, run Bridge for rendering and Structure for architecture.

If a thought resists English expression mid-workflow, read [workflow-bridge.md](./workflow-bridge.md) for the stuck passage, then return.

## When to use

- Prose is grammatically correct in the target language but feels foreign to its intended audience
- Reader says "I can't follow this" about prose that follows a coherent non-default organizational logic
- Moving between traditions: Arabic to Anglo-American, French to US business, Japanese academic to international conference, German scholarly to general readership

## Phase 1: Identify the source tradition

Read [across-languages.md](./across-languages.md) and run the organizational-logic question on the prose. Name the source tradition before proposing changes — adaptation without naming is silent normalization.

If the tradition is unclear, state which signals are present and ask the user to confirm before proceeding.

## Phase 2: Identify the target tradition

Ask if the user has not already specified:

- Who is the primary audience for this in its new form?
- What country, professional context, or register?

US academic, US business, and US journalism diverge. Identify the specific register, not just the national tradition.

Read [calibrate.md](./calibrate.md) and [fit.md](./fit.md) to map the audience to its expected conventions and to check that the adapted form will match its function in the target tradition.

## Phase 3: Map divergence

For each dimension where source and target diverge, name what the source is doing, what the target expects, and the cost of adaptation.

| Dimension | What to Compare |
|-----------|----------------|
| Organization | Parallel/circular vs. linear-deductive; associative vs. hierarchical; delayed thesis vs. thesis-first |
| Position-taking | Dialectical (hold contradiction first) vs. thesis-first; accumulated authority vs. stated upfront |
| Explicitness | High-context (implied, reader supplies) vs. low-context (stated, writer supplies) |
| Certainty | Hedged or graduated commitment vs. assertive claims |
| Elaboration | Extended scaffolding, parallel restatement, accumulative persuasion vs. economical hierarchical evidence |

## Phase 4: Adapt

Transform structure to function in the target tradition. Enforce scope.

**Moves:**

- Organization — argument sequencing, section order, thesis placement
- Positional conventions — where and how the writer's stance appears
- Explicitness — making implied content explicit (high to low context) or removing over-explanation (low to high)

Read [arc.md](./arc.md), [clarify.md](./clarify.md), and [calibrate.md](./calibrate.md) for arc redesign, resequencing, and explicitness calibration.

**Does not move:**

- Content — what is claimed, argued, or asserted
- Voice — how the writer sounds
- Vocabulary — specific word choices, register, diction
- Ideas — the intellectual substance

If adaptation pressure pushes toward changing content, stop and name the tension. The user decides whether to expand scope.

## Phase 5: Annotate

Annotation is mandatory. Adaptation without annotation is silent normalization.

Each adapted move addresses three questions:

1. What structural move was made? Be specific. Not "restructured for clarity" but "moved thesis from final paragraph to opening; converted parallel-restatement into hierarchical evidence chain; made the implied causal link in paragraph 3 explicit."
2. What did the original do that the adapted version cannot? Name the rhetorical function and its cost. Example: "Your parallel elaboration was compressed into a linear argument. The accumulative persuasion was replaced by hierarchical evidence. The reader processes faster but does not feel the weight of the repeated pattern."
3. Could the original structure be preserved with scaffolding for the target audience? Always offer this option — sometimes the move that feels foreign is the strongest part, and a brief framing note for the audience preserves it.

Read [voice.md](./voice.md) to check what positional moves and authorial stance the adaptation preserves or loses.

## NEVER

- **Never adapt content along with structure.** If ideas shift in the adaptation, that is failure.
- **Never adapt voice, vocabulary, or specific word choices unless the user explicitly asks.** Structure adapts; everything else holds.

For cross-cutting rules (hedging split, friction, gaps, L1 patterns, tradition), read [never.md](./never.md).
