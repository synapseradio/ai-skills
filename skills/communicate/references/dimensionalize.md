# Dimensionalize

Separate monolithic concepts into distinct improvable dimensions.

## Diagnostic Question

Ask: Where does feedback conflate distinct concerns?
What independent aspects hide behind "needs work" or similar labels? Can the monolithic assessment be separated into improvable dimensions?

## Instructions

1. **Detect false unity** - Identify when a concept appears as one thing but actually represents several independent aspects. Mark places where judgments collapse multiple qualities into single verdicts ("this is good", "that's unclear", "needs improvement").

2. **Separate independent axes** - Distinguish dimensions that can vary without affecting each other. Two qualities are separate when one can move while the other holds still — when something can be high on one and low on the other in either direction. Identify what aspects could be improved independently.

3. **Name dimensions precisely** - Articulate each axis with specificity. A monolithic word like "quality" covers too much to score. Pull it apart into axes whose names point to what can be observed and changed on their own.

4. **Map variation along each axis** - Show how each dimension spans a range. Each axis has a visible scale, and the thing being assessed sits somewhere on that scale — making the range explicit reveals position on each axis independently of the others.

5. **Reveal hidden trade-offs** - Dimensionalizing exposes that improving one aspect may constrain another. Pushing one axis up can pull another down — gains on one quality often arrive at the cost of a different quality. These trade-offs become visible only when dimensions are separated.

6. **Enable targeted feedback** - Transform undifferentiated judgments into dimensional assessments. State the position on each relevant axis, so the receiver knows which axis to act on and in which direction.

## Questions

A short interrogation to run when a judgment word is doing too much work. The questions name what to inspect. They don't prescribe what to conclude.

- Where am I treating a judgment word — clear, strong, good, polished, working — as a single thing when several distinct qualities are riding on it?
- For each candidate dimension, can it move while another holds still? Can something be high on this one and low on that one without contradiction?
- Does each axis have a name precise enough that a reader could observe and score it on its own, or does the name still cover too much?
- Where does each axis sit along its range, and where does the thing I'm assessing sit on each axis separately?
- What trade-offs become visible only after the dimensions separate? Where does pushing one axis up pull another down?
- If I rewrote a piece of feedback in dimensional form, what would the receiver be able to act on that they could not act on before?
- Are any axes I named secretly the same axis under two labels?

## Quality Criteria

When the dimensionalizing is sound:

- [ ] Each judgment word that names a quality has been separated into the specific axes it actually bundles.
- [ ] Each axis is named precisely enough to be observed and scored on its own.
- [ ] The axes are independent: each can move while the others hold still.
- [ ] Each axis carries a range, and the thing being assessed has a position along that range.
- [ ] Trade-offs between axes are visible — where pushing one up pulls another down.
- [ ] Feedback or verdicts are stated dimensionally, with positions on specific axes, so the receiver can act on them.
- [ ] No axis listed is a restatement of another in different words.
