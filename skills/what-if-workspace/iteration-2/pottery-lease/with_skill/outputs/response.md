**Q5 — which tiles survive?** Four combinations, none of them ruled out by anything already in motion:

- **Solo scale-up** — demand holds, but your co-teacher leaves next year. The space earns its keep, but one of you is now running an expanded footprint alone, which means hiring or a leaner schedule, not a clean continuation of today.

None of these contradicts anything you've said — they're kept on the board on consistency, not on how comfortable they feel.

**Q6 — does every plausible future land in exactly one tile?** Yes. Demand either holds or it doesn't; she either stays or she goes. No future needs a fifth tile, and none double-books two of these four.

**Q7 — what follows from what, inside each tile?** In *Full steam*, more capacity supports more revenue, which opens the door to a part-time hire down the line rather than forcing one. That third move is the one that holds up everywhere, so Q10 doesn't need to fire — this isn't a case of waiting for more information, it's a case of shaping the commitment so the information you're missing can't hurt you as much once it arrives.
