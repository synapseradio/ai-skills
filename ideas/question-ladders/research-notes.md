## Corrections to question-ladders.idea.md

- The core claim "each question presupposes the previous answer" splits in two under erotetic logic: backward presupposition (a question rests on what is already established) and forward licensing, or erotetic implication (an answer makes new questions askable). QUD theory adds that rungs bond to the driving question, not to adjacent rungs — a question earns its place by partially answering the question under discussion, not by chaining to its neighbor.
- The six-types Socratic list is R.W. Paul's earlier taxonomy, known mostly through derivative handouts. The Paul & Elder criticalthinking.org guide itself taxonomizes by eight elements of thought.
- Hintikka's terms are principal vs. *operative* questions, and interrogative vs. deductive moves.
- The Gottman line softens to: how conflict is conducted and repaired predicts outcomes, not conflict itself. Anger alone is non-predictive (1994 background); the 1998 study finds contempt, belligerence, defensiveness, and harsh startup predicting separation. The repair-attempts claim lives in Gottman & Silver 1999 (trade) and The Marriage Clinic 1999 (clinical, via Weiss 1980 "sentiment override"); the dedicated observational study is Gottman, Driver & Tabares 2015, which ties repair success to its *reception*, not the attempt's quality.
- "Simplest counterexample": mathematical practice supports the *minimal counterexample* as a proof technique — the "minimal criminal": assume a smallest breaking case and derive a contradiction (Chartrand et al.; Courant & Robbins on smallest bad maps) — not as a documented heuristic norm of counterexample search. State the analogy with that boundary.
- QUD completeness: Ginzburg (The Interactive Stance, 2012) and Roberts both use QUD, but differently — Ginzburg's is a partially ordered set on per-participant dialogue gameboards, Roberts's a totally ordered shared stack. Cite both; the skill's rung test follows Roberts.
- Gentner's structure-mapping warns that the Jacobian→Jacob name chain is a surface match — a retrieval cue, not validity evidence.
- Bloom's low-to-high sequencing is contested; only the weak form (a question can presuppose missing ground) is defensible. The skill's ordering reference encodes presupposition, not difficulty, as the constraint.
- Established, not speculative: the pretending question is auditable and owes an audit; what is granted at the start is constitutively unauditable — its only rigor is being named and chosen (hinges; charity; Agrippa; Carroll).

- **Question presupposition** — Belnap & Steel 1976; Wiśniewski (Inferential Erotetic Logic); van Fraassen 1980 (a question *arises* vs. has no answer).

- **Interrogative model** — Hintikka & Harris 1988.
- **Laddering** — Reynolds & Gutman 1988; Grunert & Grunert 1995 (soft/hard laddering); Veludo-de-Oliveira et al. 2006 (failure modes and remedies).
- **Socratic probes** — R.W. Paul (six types); Paul & Elder, The Art of Socratic Questioning (elements of thought); Padesky 2019 (guide discovery, don't change minds).
- **Ladder of inference** — Argyris; Senge, The Fifth Discipline Fieldbook; The Systems Thinker ("explain the steps in your thinking").
- **Sequence shapes** — interviewing literature (funnel, inverted funnel, tunnel); Fisher & Geiselman (cognitive interview).

- **Transfer** — Gentner 1983 (systematicity); Novick 1988; Holyoak & Koh 1987.

- **Grants** — Wittgenstein, On Certainty §§115, 341–343; Quine 1960 / Davidson 1974 (charity); Sextus Empiricus (Agrippa's five modes) / Hans Albert 1968; Carroll 1895 (the tortoise).
