# Expression Plugin: Information Architecture Research

Research landscape for reorganizing the expression plugin's reference material. Synthesized from three parallel research tracks.

## Current State

21 reference files in 5 categories + 1 meta-lens:

| Category | Count | References |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Clarity | 6 | clarify, activate, strengthen, illustrate, omit, ground |
| Integrity | 3 | signal-confidence, bound-scope, surface-assumptions |
| Narrative | 6 | arc, rhythm, voice, diction, tone, position |
| Generative | 3 | extract-implications, dimensionalize, pose-questions |
| Connection | 2 | calibrate, bridge |
| Meta-lens | 1 | tradition |

Each reference is accessed via diagnostic question → technique file. The index organizes by category, each category contains diagnostic questions that point to technique files.

## Track 1: Anglo-American Craft Book Organization

Seven major craft books use **five distinct organizational strategies**:

| Strategy | Books | Decomposes by |
|----------|-------|--------------|
| Grammatical level (word → sentence → paragraph → document) | Strunk & White, Tufte | Syntactic unit size |
| Prose quality (clarity, cohesion, concision, elegance) | Williams | Named quality as isolable skill |
| Abstraction funnel (principles → methods → genres → attitudes) | Zinsser | Level of specificity |
| Practitioner workflow (decisions in editing order) | Dreyer | Task sequence |
| Narrative arc (becoming a writer) | King | Biographical chronology |
| Anti-structure (continuous aphoristic flow) | Klinkenborg | Refusal to decompose |

**Convergences across all books:**

- Sentence as primary unit of concern
- Clarity, concision, active voice, concrete language as shared values
- Revision as central to process

**Gaps in the Anglo-American tradition:**

- No book organizes by **cognitive operation** (generate, select, arrange, revise)
- No book organizes by **reader effect** (create suspense, build trust, convey authority)
- Classical rhetoric's five canons absent as explicit framework

### Detailed Book Structures

**Strunk & White** — Rules → Principles → Style (ascending grammatical scope)
**Zinsser** — Principles → Methods → Forms (genre-specific) → Attitudes (psychology)
**Dreyer** — Active craft decisions (front) → Reference lookup (back)
**King** — Memoir structure with "Toolbox" metaphor (vocabulary on top, style underneath, form at bottom)
**Tufte** — Pure syntactic taxonomy (14 chapters, one per grammatical construction)
**Williams** — Diagnostic-to-therapeutic progression: clarity → cohesion → coherence → concision → elegance
**Klinkenborg** — No chapters, no sections; continuous aphoristic flow rejecting decomposition

## Track 2: Cross-Cultural Writing Traditions

### Arabic Balagha (Eloquence/Rhetoric)

Three interconnected sciences — **not stages but simultaneous dimensions**:

| Science | Covers | Principle |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| ʿilm al-Maʿānī (Meanings) | Semantic appropriateness; whether meaning fits communicative context | Is this the right meaning for this moment? |
| ʿilm al-Bayān (Exposition) | Modes of expression; figurative language; expressive choices | Multiple ways to express the same meaning, each with different weight |
| ʿilm al-Badīʿ (Innovation) | Elegant devices; parallelism; wordplay; allusion | Beauty and perfection beyond necessity |

**Progression:** Meaning → Clarity → Beauty (recursive, not sequential)

**Key difference from Anglo-American:** Focuses on the text as finished object, not the writer's process. Primacy of semantic appropriateness over universal rules.

### Classical Rhetoric (Five Canons)

Sequential production stages:

1. **Inventio** — Finding ideas, arguments, proofs
2. **Dispositio** — Ordering material
3. **Elocutio** — Expressing in appropriate language (3 levels: plain, middle, grand)
4. **Memoria** — Retaining and retrieving
5. **Pronuntiatio** — Performance and delivery

Quintilian gives 3 books to Style but only 1 each to Memory and Delivery — style is the primary technical challenge.

### French Tradition

- **Commentaire composé:** Thematic analysis organized into coherent axes (not chronological)
- **Dissertation:** Thesis-driven argumentative structure in three parts
- **Oulipo:** Constraints as generative engines — inverts the rule-based approach entirely

### Japanese Aesthetic Tradition

- **Bunshō Tokuhon** (writing craft readers): Craft taught through exemplary readings, not abstract rules
- **Tanizaki's aesthetics:** Suggestion over statement; incompleteness as perfection; emptiness as presence
- Emphasis on **aesthetic principles and linguistic properties** rather than technical rules

### German Tradition

- **Adorno's "The Essay as Form":** Anti-systematic; the essay thinks through particulars without predetermined closure
- Constellational form; refusal of identity thinking; exemplarity over abstraction

### Latin American Tradition

- **Borges:** Tradition as available toolbox, not constraint; world literature as material
- **Piglia:** "The classic short story always tells two stories" — dual narrative layers
- Emphasis on intellectual freedom and experimental commitment

### Cross-Cultural Synthesis

| Tradition | Asks | Organizes by |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| Arabic balagha | How does meaning become eloquent? | Semantic-expressive-aesthetic progression |
| Classical rhetoric | How do I persuade this audience? | Sequential production stages |
| French pedagogy | How do I analyze/develop ideas coherently? | Structural-thematic analysis |
| Oulipo | How can constraints force creativity? | Formal problems to solve |
| Japanese aesthetics | What principles guide aesthetic rightness? | Aesthetic principles |
| Adorno/German | How can thought remain free in form? | Anti-systematic critique |

**Core finding:** Anglo-American decontextualized rules are the outlier. Most traditions organize by context, purpose, and aesthetic principle.

## Track 3: Academic/Theoretical Frameworks

### Six Major Taxonomies

| Framework | Axis | Grain | Unit |
|-----------|------|-------|------|
| Classical Rhetoric (5 canons) | Production sequence | Macro | Cognitive acts |
| Flower & Hayes (cognitive process) | Mental operations | Macro | Planning → Translating → Reviewing |
| Process Pedagogy (Elbow/Murray) | Psychological modes | Macro | Generative vs. evaluative stance |
| Assessment Rubrics (6+1 Traits, IELTS) | Writing quality dimensions | Mixed | Scorable traits |
| Stylistics (Leech & Short) | Linguistic features | Micro-to-macro | Lexical → grammatical → syntactic → discourse |
| Genre Theory (Miller, Bakhtin) | Social function | Macro | Typified rhetorical action |

### 6+1 Trait Model (most relevant to plugin organization)

| Trait | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| Ideas | Main message; content; specificity |
| Organization | Logical structure; flow; progression |
| Voice | Authorial presence; tone; personality |
| Word Choice | Vocabulary selection; precision |
| Sentence Fluency | Variety; rhythm; smoothness |
| Conventions | Grammar, spelling, punctuation |
| Presentation | Visual design, formatting |

### Key Tension: Process vs. Product

- Classical Rhetoric, Flower & Hayes, Elbow/Murray → what the writer **does**
- Assessment Rubrics, Stylistics → what the text **is**
- Genre Theory → mediates (product forms meaningful only in social context)

### Nested Hierarchy of Writing Operations

```
Genre/Social Function
  → Communicative Purpose
    → Text-Level Organization
      → Cognitive Processes
        → Sentence/Paragraph Structure
          → Lexical/Grammatical Choices
            → Surface Conventions
```

Each level constrains and enables levels above and below.

## Implications for Expression Plugin

### What the current organization does well

1. **Diagnostic questions as entry points** — echoes Williams's diagnostic-therapeutic structure
2. **Named techniques with clear scope** — each file has a bounded job
3. **Category grouping** — the 5 categories (Clarity, Integrity, Narrative, Generative, Connection) provide navigable structure
4. **Tradition meta-lens** — directly addresses the cross-cultural finding that context matters

### Questions the research raises

1. **Are the 5 categories the right decomposition?** The research shows many competing axes: grammatical level, prose quality, production stage, cognitive operation, social function, aesthetic dimension
2. **Should techniques map to operations, qualities, or something else?** Current mix: some are operations (activate, strengthen, illustrate), some are qualities (rhythm, voice, tone), some are analytical moves (surface-assumptions, extract-implications)
3. **What's missing?** No coverage of: invention/generation, arrangement/structure (beyond arc), revision as process, genre awareness, reader effect
4. **What's redundant?** voice/tone/diction cluster tightly; position overlaps with voice
5. **Is the diagnostic question model the right access pattern?** Alternatives: workflow sequence, quality checklist, linguistic level progression

### Organizational Models to Consider

1. **Williams-style quality progression:** clarity → cohesion → coherence → concision → elegance (ascending scope)
2. **Arabic balagha dimensions:** meaning → expression → beauty (recursive, simultaneous)
3. **6+1 Traits adaptation:** ideas, organization, voice, word choice, fluency, conventions
4. **Hybrid:** Keep diagnostic questions but regroup by axis (what the text does vs. what the writer does vs. what the reader experiences)
