1. Map the system: components, relationships, and feedback loops (reinforcing and balancing).
2. Locate bottlenecks where things get stuck.
3. Find amplification points where one action affects many outcomes.
4. Spot compounding opportunities — what builds on itself over time?
5. Identify root causes that produce many symptoms.
6. Evaluate leverage ratio: favor points where a small change shifts the whole system over points that need constant effort to maintain.
7. Name the single highest-leverage intervention.
