1. State what is being examined.
2. Identify the relevant part-whole relations — components, members, portions, features, stages — selecting only the decomposition that fits.
3. Find the natural joints where boundaries already exist.
4. Verify coverage (do the parts account for the whole?) and connections (how do parts relate?).
5. Recurse into parts that are still too complex.
6. Stop when parts are simple enough to reason about individually.
