# Carry the Ladder Across

Move a ladder to a new domain on its relations, never on its vocabulary.

## Diagnostic Question

Ask: Am I carrying this ladder to the new domain because its relations hold there, or because the words match?

## Instructions

1. **Transfer on relations, not vocabulary.** A ladder transfers when the relations between its rungs hold in the new domain — what establishes what, what audits what, what licenses the next question. Shared terminology proves none of that.

2. **Name the relations explicitly.** To claim a transfer, write out the relations the ladder depends on — "this rung's answer establishes the presupposition of that one," "this question audits that simplification" — and test each relation in the new domain, one by one, against the record in hand. A relation the record cannot settle becomes itself the next question to ask. The caveats travel with the output — what you hand back names which relations hold and which don't.

3. **Demote surface resemblance to a cue.** Shared words and similar-looking objects are at best a reason to *consider* the analogy, never evidence that it's valid. Surface-matched, structure-mismatched analogies are the standard way transfer goes wrong: the resemblance retrieves the ladder, and then the ladder gets trusted on the strength of the retrieval.

4. **Transfer a flat set on its presuppositions and coverage.** A question set without rung relations still carries assumptions and a claim of coverage; test those where a ladder would test its relations.

5. **Branch on the outcome.** Relations hold → carry the ladder. Some hold → carry those rungs and rebuild the rest ([ladder](./ladder.md)). None hold → rebuild from scratch. Unsettled → the uncertainty is the first question you ask.

## Questions

- Which relations carry this ladder — named explicitly, not gestured at?
- Does each relation hold in the new domain, tested there rather than assumed?
- What drew me to this analogy: the relations, or shared words and lookalike objects?
- If the vocabulary were stripped out, would the mapping still stand?

## Quality Criteria

When the transfer is doing its work:

- [ ] The load-bearing relations are written out, not implied.
- [ ] Each relation was tested in the new domain individually.
- [ ] Surface resemblance was treated as a retrieval cue only, never as validity evidence.
- [ ] The claim of transfer names which relations hold and which don't — partial transfers are stated as partial.
